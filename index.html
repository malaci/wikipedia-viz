About

The Project

The Wikipedia Visualization Project is currently being developed at Prof. Ron Bekkerman's Data Science Lab at the University of Haifa, Israel.
To the best of our knowledge, this work is the first-ever attempt to represent the entire Wikipedia in one interactive image, for the purpose of overviewing the content of the most prominent knowledge base of the world.
Our methodology can be used for assessing comprehensiveness of Wikipedia and defining the strategy of turning it into the complete collective memory of the humanity.

The Graph

The graph shows 443 largest categories covering over 90% of Wikipedia pages.
Two categories are connected to each other if a substantial amount of Wikipedia pages belong to both categories simultaneously.
The color of the category represents one of four main themes of Wikipedia (Places and Nature, Sports, Science and Society, Arts and Culture) or Other if the category is ambiguous.
Size of a category node corresponds to the size of the category (in number of pages it covers).
Hover over a node to see the name of the corresponding category and names of its neighbors. Click on a node to see the second-level categories and their connections.
